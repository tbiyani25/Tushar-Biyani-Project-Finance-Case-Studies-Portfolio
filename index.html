Project Finance & Infrastructure Modelling  |  Energy Transition

Developing financial models for large-scale infrastructure and energy transition assets

I have a strong interest in project finance within the energy transition, where structuring bankable, risk-allocated financing is critical to moving infrastructure projects from concept to design to delivery. Through my experience analysing large-scale infrastructure projects, I have seen that despite strong capital availability, many fail to progress due to challenges in achieving viable financing structures and aligned risk pricing.

With a strong technical and commercial background, I build financial models for large-scale infrastructure and energy projects to assess bankability, evaluate financing structures, and identify the key drivers of investment decisions.

About

Background & expertise

I am a Chartered Chemical Engineer with experience across large-scale energy and infrastructure projects, combining technical depth with commercial and financial insight. I have advised investors, developers, regulators, and project sponsors across the full capital project lifecycle within both the public and private sectors. My experience includes evaluating hydrogen pipeline FEED projects for Ofgem to inform public funding decisions, assessing the readiness and financial viability of nine UKRI-funded low-carbon projects, and supporting the development of Great British Energy's capital deployment strategy aligned to its policy objectives and risk thresholds.

I have a structured, investor-oriented approach to problem solving, with experience spanning strategy, policy and regulatory frameworks, due diligence, transaction support, and technical assessment. I am particularly interested in how projects are structured and financed to enable delivery, and in applying integrated technical and financial analysis to support investment decisions in the energy transition. This page highlights selected financial models and transaction analyses across the energy and infrastructure sector, with a focus on transaction structuring, financing considerations, and the key drivers of project bankability.
